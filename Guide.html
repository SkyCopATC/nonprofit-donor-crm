1. About This Tool

A working donor management platform for nonprofits with anywhere from 25 to 5,000 active donors. It handles every step of the donor relationship — from logging a first gift to running a five-year capital campaign pledge to filing Form 990 Schedule B at year end.

Most small nonprofits track donors in a spreadsheet that ages out the moment a key staff or board member leaves. The institutional memory walks out the door: who attended the gala, who pledged five years and is now in year three, who asked about planned giving, why a foundation gave restricted to one program. This tool is the boring infrastructure that protects all of it.

The cardinal rule

Every meaningful interaction gets logged. Not the spam emails — the real conversations. If a board member runs into a donor at the grocery store and learns their daughter just graduated, that goes in here. That's how relationships compound.

2. Getting Started

Demo credentials: demo@example.org / demo — fully populated with 6 donors, 11 gifts, an active pledge, recurring sustainer, 3 appeals, communications, and a sample stewardship plan.

Your first 30 minutes

  1. In Settings, set your EIN, mailing address, and signatory — these flow into every acknowledgment letter.
  2. Enter your top 25 donors first. Don't try to migrate everyone at once.
  3. Tag donors as you enter them (Major Donor, Board Member, Monthly Donor, etc.). Tags drive segmentation and reports.
  4. Define your current appeals. Each gift gets tagged to one — that's what lets you measure campaign performance.
  5. Log this year's gifts. Gift history drives LYBUNT, retention, tier breakdowns.
  6. Start a stewardship plan for your top 5 donors. Click + Seed standard 7-touch plan for an instant template.

4. Gifts, Pledges & Recurring Giving

Gifts

Every recorded transaction. Captures donor, date, amount, payment method, appeal, designation, acknowledgment status, and notes. Gifts roll up into the donor's lifetime total and feed every report.

Pledges vs. Recurring Giving

PledgeRecurring Gift
A signed commitment to give a defined total over a fixed timeframe — capital campaigns, multi-year major gifts.An open-ended monthly/quarterly/annual auto-renewing gift — the "sustainer" model.

Appeals

Your fundraising campaigns. Define each appeal (Annual Fund, Year-End, Gala, Capital Campaign, etc.) with type, dates, goal, and status. Goal-vs-raised progress bars update live as gifts come in.

5. Stewardship & Communications

Log every meaningful donor touchpoint — calls, lunches, emails, in-person conversations. Best practice for any donor giving $1,000+: a formal stewardship plan with at least seven non-solicitation touchpoints per year.

The standard 7-touch plan

  1. Thank-you call within 48 hours of a gift (ED, not staff)
  2. Handwritten note from a board member or beneficiary
  3. Mid-year impact update (1 page, specific outcomes, no ask)
  4. Site visit or program experience
  5. Annual report mailed with personalized cover letter
  6. Birthday or anniversary card
  7. Year-end thank-you (separate from year-end appeal)

6. Tax Acknowledgments

The IRS requires a contemporaneous written acknowledgment for any single contribution of $250 or more. Best practice is to thank every gift, regardless of size.

Required elements (IRS Pub 1771)

  • Name of the organization
  • Amount of cash contribution OR description (not value) of non-cash contribution
  • Statement on goods/services provided (or "no goods or services")
  • Tax-deductibility statement
  • Organization's EIN (recommended)
Quid pro quo gifts (gala tickets, auction items)

You MUST disclose the fair market value of goods/services exchanged. The deductible portion is the gift amount minus the FMV.

The Tax Acknowledgments page generates compliant letters from your boilerplate. Preview as HTML or download Word .docx. Bulk action generates ALL pending acknowledgments in one document.

7. Reports & Annual Donor Report

LYBUNT & SYBUNT

LYBUNT (Last Year But Unfortunately Not This year) — donors who gave last year but haven't given this year. Your highest-value reactivation targets.

SYBUNT (Some Year But Unfortunately Not This year) — donors who gave in earlier years but neither last year nor this year.

Retention rate

The percentage of last year's donors who gave again this year. Industry benchmark for small orgs is around 45%. Above 60% is excellent.

Form 990 Schedule B

Donors over $5,000 in a year must typically be disclosed on Schedule B of Form 990. The Reports page surfaces this list automatically.

Build Annual Donor Report

Generates a polished, board-ready report (HTML preview + .docx download) with executive summary, top donors, appeal performance, donor composition, retention narrative, and year-ahead goals.

Administrator Access

The sign-in screen has an Administrator Access link below the Sign In button. Use it to sign in as Administrator with just a password — no email needed. This is a per-browser admin role; the password is stored only on the current computer.

  • First time: Click Administrator Access. You'll see a "First-time setup" prompt with two password fields — enter a password (6+ characters) and confirm it. Click Create Admin Password.
  • Subsequent times: Click Administrator Access, enter that same password, and click Enter Admin Panel.
  • Once signed in as Administrator, you'll land on the dashboard with full admin privileges, including the Admin page in the sidebar (visibility into all teams, users, and activity stored in this browser).
  • Click ← Back to regular sign-in at the bottom of the admin panel to return to the normal email/password form.

Note: the admin password is unique to each browser. If you set it up at home and then visit the app on a work computer, you'll see the first-time-setup prompt again. To grant admin access on a new machine, register a regular user account or set up a fresh admin password there.
