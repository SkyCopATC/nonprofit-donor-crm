Donor CRM

Stop tracking donors in spreadsheets. Manage relationships from first gift through major-donor cultivation — directory, gifts, pledges, recurring giving, appeals, stewardship plans, IRS-compliant acknowledgments, and a polished annual donor report.

What is this tool?

A working donor management platform for nonprofits with anywhere from 25 to 5,000 active donors. It handles every step of the donor relationship — from logging a first gift to running a five-year capital campaign pledge to filing Form 990 Schedule B at year end.

Most small nonprofits track donors in a spreadsheet that ages out the moment a key staff or board member leaves. The institutional memory walks out the door: who attended the gala, who pledged five years and is now in year three, who asked about planned giving, why a foundation gave restricted to one program. This tool is the boring infrastructure that protects all of it.

The cardinal rule: Every meaningful interaction gets logged. Not the spam emails — the real conversations. If a board member runs into a donor at the grocery store and learns their daughter just graduated, that goes in here. That's how relationships compound.

Getting started

  1. Sign up or sign in. Demo: demo@example.org / demo — fully populated with 6 donors, 11 gifts, an active pledge, a recurring sustainer, 3 appeals, communications, and a sample stewardship plan.
  2. In Settings, set your EIN, mailing address, and signatory — these flow into every acknowledgment letter.
  3. Enter your top 25 donors first. Don't try to migrate everyone at once.
  4. Tag donors as you enter them (Major Donor, Board Member, Monthly Donor, etc.). Tags drive segmentation and reports.
  5. Define your current Appeals. Each gift gets tagged to one — that's what lets you measure campaign performance.
  6. Log this year's gifts from each donor. Gift history drives LYBUNT, retention, tier breakdowns.
  7. Start a stewardship plan for your top 5 donors. Click + Seed standard 7-touch plan for an instant template.

Recommended weekly workflow

  1. Monday — Gift log. Enter all gifts received last week. Mark which need acknowledgment.
  2. Monday — Thank-you letters. In Tax Acknowledgments, generate .docx letters for every unacked gift. Mail same day.
  3. Tuesday — Communications. Log meaningful donor conversations from the week (calls, lunches, emails, hallway chats).
  4. Wednesday — Stewardship review. Check upcoming touchpoints on the dashboard. Make the calls and write the notes today.
  5. Thursday — Pipeline review. Spend 30 minutes on the kanban. Who's ready to move stages? Who's stuck?
  6. Friday — Follow-ups. Clear or reschedule each "Follow-ups due" item on the dashboard.
If you do nothing else, do Monday. Acknowledging gifts within a week is the single biggest retention lever in fundraising research.

Donor Directory

The roster of every individual, family, foundation, and corporate donor. Each profile captures contact info, donor type, segmentation tags, spouse/partner, interests, notes, DNS (Do Not Solicit) flag, pipeline stage, and computed lifetime giving. Click any donor row for the full detail page with embedded gift history, pledges, recurring giving, communications log, and stewardship plan.

Segmentation tags

Tags are multi-select. The same donor can be tagged Major Donor + Board Member + Volunteer. Common tags include Major Donor, Monthly Donor, Lapsed, Board Member, Foundation, Corporate, Planned Giving Prospect, Anonymous, and DNS. Tags drive filtering in the directory and segmentation in reports.

Major Donor Pipeline (Moves Management)

A kanban for prospects you're actively cultivating toward a major gift ask. Not for every donor in your file — only those for whom you're making moves toward a specific solicitation.

The 1-2-3 rule. Most experienced fundraisers say it takes 1 great meeting, 2 substantive follow-ups, and 3 months minimum from Qualification to a successful Solicitation. Don't rush.

Gifts

Every recorded transaction. Captures donor, date, amount, payment method (Check, Credit Card, Stock, DAF, IRA QCD, In-Kind, etc.), appeal, designation, acknowledgment status, and notes. Gifts automatically roll up into the donor's lifetime total, drive tier calculations, and feed every report.

Designation matters. "Unrestricted" is best for the organization. "Restricted - [purpose]" legally obligates you to spend that money on that purpose only. Track carefully and reconcile annually.

Pledges

A signed commitment to give a defined total over a fixed timeframe — capital campaign pledges, multi-year major gifts. Captures total amount, start/end date, frequency (annual/quarterly/monthly), installment size, remaining balance, signed-agreement flag, and notes. Progress bar shows paid vs. outstanding.

Recurring Giving

Open-ended monthly/quarterly/annual auto-renewing gifts — the "sustainer" or "monthly donor" model. Different from a pledge: no defined end, no signed total commitment. Captures amount, frequency, start date, status (active/paused/cancelled), and method. The page surfaces predictable monthly revenue at a glance.

Each actual transaction should also be logged as a Gift when it processes. This page tracks the commitment; the Gifts page tracks the transactions.

Appeals

Your fundraising campaigns. Define each appeal (Annual Fund, Spring Gala, Year-End, Capital Campaign, Giving Tuesday, etc.) with type, dates, goal, and status. Each gift is tagged to an appeal so you can measure performance. Goal-vs-raised progress bars and gift counts update live.

💡 Appeals defined here appear in the Gift entry dropdown and roll up to your overall fundraising strategy in the Fundraising & Development demo.

Communications

Log every meaningful donor touchpoint — calls, lunches, emails, in-person conversations. Captures date, channel, subject, summary, who logged it, and a follow-up next-step with date. The dashboard surfaces all due follow-ups so nothing falls through the cracks.

Stewardship Plans

A per-donor map of touchpoints to keep top supporters engaged. Best practice: a formal plan for every $1,000+ donor with at least seven non-solicitation touchpoints per year.

The standard 7-touch plan

  1. Thank-you call within 48 hours of a gift (ED, not a staff member)
  2. Handwritten note from a board member or beneficiary
  3. Mid-year impact update (1 page, specific outcomes, no ask)
  4. Site visit or program experience
  5. Annual report mailed with personalized cover letter
  6. Birthday or anniversary card
  7. Year-end thank-you (separate from the year-end appeal)

Click + Seed standard 7-touch plan on any new plan to generate this template, then customize dates and notes.

Tax Acknowledgments

The IRS requires a contemporaneous written acknowledgment for any single contribution of $250 or more. Best practice is to thank every gift, regardless of size.

Required elements (IRS Pub 1771)

  • Name of the organization
  • Amount of cash contribution OR description (but not value) of non-cash contribution
  • Statement that no goods or services were provided, OR a description and good-faith estimate of the value of goods/services provided
  • Statement that the contribution is tax-deductible to the extent allowed by law
  • The org's EIN (recommended)

The Tax Acknowledgments page generates compliant letters from your boilerplate. Preview as HTML or download Word .docx. The bulk action generates all pending acknowledgments in a single document and auto-marks every gift acknowledged.

Quid pro quo gifts (gala tickets, auction items). If you provided goods or services in exchange for the gift, you MUST disclose the fair market value of those goods or services. The deductible portion is the gift amount MINUS the FMV. Update boilerplate accordingly for gala-style appeals.

Reports

Live computed reports for board, funders, and IRS reporting:

LYBUNT — Last Year But Unfortunately Not This year

Donors who gave last year but haven't yet given this year. Your highest-value reactivation targets. Most can be saved with a personal call from the ED or board chair.

SYBUNT — Some Year But Unfortunately Not This year

Donors who gave in earlier years but neither last year nor this year. Lower hit rate than LYBUNT but worth a focused Q1 reactivation campaign annually.

Retention rate

The percentage of last year's donors who gave again this year. Industry benchmark for small orgs is around 45%. Above 60% is excellent. Below 30% means stewardship is broken.

Build Annual Donor Report

Generates a polished, board-ready report. Includes:

  • Executive summary with year-over-year growth
  • KPI dashboard (raised, donors, retention, average gift)
  • Top 15 donors by lifetime giving
  • Appeal performance: goal vs. raised, % of goal
  • Donor composition by type (individual / foundation / corporate / etc.)
  • Retention & reactivation narrative (LYBUNT, SYBUNT)
  • Stewardship completion rate
  • Major donor pipeline outlook
  • Year-ahead goals

Two outputs: HTML preview (print-ready in a browser window) and .docx download (Word format, ready to drop into a board packet).

Legal & compliance notes

Things this tool helps you stay on top of but doesn't replace professional guidance for:

  • IRS substantiation (Pub 1771). Written acknowledgment required for any gift $250+. Letter must be contemporaneous (received before the donor files their return).
  • Quid pro quo disclosures. Gifts over $75 with goods/services in exchange require disclosure of the FMV of those goods/services in writing.
  • Restricted gifts. Once you accept a restricted gift, you have a legal obligation to use it for the stated purpose. Track designations carefully and document any donor-approved redirections.
  • Form 990 Schedule B. Donors over $5,000 in a year must typically be disclosed (with redacted names on the public copy). Confirm filing requirements with your accountant.
  • State charitable solicitation registration. If you solicit donors in multiple states, most states require you to register. The Compliance Tracker demo flags this.
  • Data privacy. Donor PII is sensitive. Honor every DNS flag. For production data, you need encrypted backup, role-based permissions, and audit logging — Bloomerang, Little Green Light, Network for Good, and DonorPerfect are common small-shop choices beyond this demo.
